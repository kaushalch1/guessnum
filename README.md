I built this game as a fun way to test your intuition (and maybe your luck). It’s a simple but addictive guessing game where the goal is to find a hidden number between 1 and 100. It’s perfect for a quick brain break or a head-to-head challenge with a friend.

 How to play:https://kaushalch1.github.io/guessnum/
Choose your challenge:

Single Player: It’s you only.try to find the number in the lowest possible.

Single player vs bot: the player willll choose the number after the player chooses the number the bot will guesss its number.there are 2 different modes.Fastest one wins.The player aand bot are given 2 different numbers.

2-Player Mode: This turns it into a real competition. Two players take turns guessing, and the game tracks who gets closer or hits the mark first!

The Goal: Enter a number between 1 and 100. The game will tell you if you're "Too High" or "Too Low" until someone nails it.

Why I made this
I wanted to build something that felt interactive and competitive but stayed very lightweight. The real challenge for me here was building the logic for the 2-player mode—making sure the game accurately switches between players and keeps the "secret number" hidden from both until the very end. I'm currently working on adding even more modes (like a "Time Attack" or "Limited Lives" mode)y!

The Tech behind the game
I kept the stack simple so I could focus on the game mechanics:

HTML5: Used for the game interface and input fields.

CSS3: I styled this to be clean and responsive, so it looks good on a phone or a laptop.

JavaScript: I handled all the game logic myself. I used Math.random() to generate the secret numbers and wrote the conditional logic that gives players feedback on their guesses.
